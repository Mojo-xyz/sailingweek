01 · The Model
01 · The Model
Warm Water. Trade Winds. Serious Fun.
Racing by day, food and music by night, a destination that fills up because of it — the warm-water regatta is a proven format. The Red Sea has the water, the wind and the venue for it, and SSF 2026 showed it works. This is how big it gets.

02 · The Week
02 · The Week
Five Days That Build.
Boats arrive over the days before. Racing stays compact so skippers don't lose a working week. The festival wraps around it — opening with a fleet arrival, peaking on Festival Day, closing with the Somabay Cup.
Day 0–1
Arrival & Opening
The fleet lands and rigs on the beach. The passage fleet sails in from El Gouna. Opening ceremony on the dock, then the first big night.
Arrivals · Feeder · Opening party
Day 2–3
Racing & Demo Day
Cruisers offshore, dinghies racing in front of the beach. Anyone can try sailing, wing or kite. Village and yacht showcase open.
Racing · Try-sailing · Village
Day 4 · Festival Day
Everything at Once
No scored racing. Every class on the water together — sail, kite, wing, windsurf. Village at full strength, families on the beach, music on the sand.
Parade · Public peak
Day 5
Finals & the Cup
Short finals close to shore. The Somabay Cup and the closing party — the biggest night of the week.
Finals · Cup · Closing
Every day
The Village
Food, vendors, talks, young sailors, brand activations and live music. What keeps the 95% who aren't racing here all week.
Food · Talks · Music
Every night
A Different Venue
Barbouni, Kaktus, Wyte, the marina — the after-party moves. The week tours the destination instead of parking in one bar.
Beach nights · Prizegivings
03 · Beyond the Racing
03 · Beyond the Racing
Food, Music, and the Sea.
The nights, the food and the village are what turn a regatta into a week people don't want to leave — and it's the part Somabay is built for. The racing is the backdrop.
Nights that move
A different Somabay outlet each evening. Opening party on the dock, six nights later the closing party and the Cup.
Food & music
Local kitchens, a stage from afternoon into the evening, dance — the kind of night people come back for.
Come and try
Free demo sailing, wing and kite on the beach. The entry point for anyone who's never been on the water.
For most people, the nights are the event. This is what we're best placed to do.

The showcase
A boat show inside the week.
FX Yachting joins with its fleet — and this is why the timing matters: those yachts are in the Red Sea in March, heading north, and gone by May. Alongside them, Red Sea Sails brings Hanse, Fountaine Pajot, Lagoon and X-Yachts, with Felix Maritime as technical partner — boats in the water, booths on the dock, sea trials through the week. A yacht buyer and a Somabay property buyer are frequently the same person; the showcase puts both in front of the same audience.

Option A
23–28
March
Into the European Easter break
  • Catches the bluewater fleet heading north through the Red Sea before the Suez transit — gone by May
  • A rally brings a fleet up the coast to Egypt in late March; SSW right after gives them a reason to stay
  • FX Yachting's fleet is in the Red Sea in March, not May — their boats can be part of the experience
  • Shoulder season — fills a quiet week rather than a full one
Option B
Eid
al-Adha
Mid-May, subject to the lunar calendar
  • FX Yachting's luxury fleet is in the Red Sea in March, not May — their yachts are essential to the experience
  • A rally brings a fleet up the coast to Egypt that same window — a chance to have them stop and stay for the week
  • Kicks off the marine season — and puts Somabay's new marina in front of the world the moment it opens
Both sit against long holidays. We'd want to check either against Somabay's own calendar before fixing anything.
